What it is

A single repository that bundles several browser demos exploring the Distributed Reconstruction framework. The bundle includes the cellular biology layer (HPA-axis cell graph), the harmonic solver, the generator/scanner pair, the BWV 847 pipeline, the supernova solver, the rank-sphere viewer, and a city / power-grid pair. Every demo is a self-contained HTML file with no build step, no server, and no external dependencies beyond a modern browser.

This repository is, in effect, the early sandbox where the framework was first explored visually before the v0.1 paper was written. The bundled files are dated 2026-03-23 (with a couple of rank-sphere variants from 2026-03-01) and represent a snapshot of how the ideas were being prototyped at that moment. Several of these demos have since graduated into their own dedicated repositories with cleaner code and updated framing — those canonical live versions are linked in the companion section below. The bundle stays around as a historical record of the exploration phase.

Why it matters — bio digital twins

A bio digital twin is a computational model of a specific biological system — a section of cortex, a hepatic lobule, a heart chamber — wired tightly enough that it can predict the system’s response to a stimulus. Building a useful twin requires several different kinds of geometric and computational machinery to work together: cellular dynamics on graph topology, sparse forward and backward projections, multi-vantage-point reconstruction, encode/process/decode loops on ranked substrates. No single demo covers all of these; each demo isolates one slice.

The encoder-brain bundle is the explorer’s index for that work. A reader can open the landing page, pick a slice, run the demo for it, and see one specific piece of the larger machinery in isolation. The bundled snapshots predate the canonical live versions in the per-demo repositories, so the framing and the styling are rougher; the value is in the breadth of slices visible in one place.

For a reader looking at the bio digital twin direction for the first time, the encoder-brain bundle is the fastest way to scan the landscape. For a reader who knows what they want, the canonical live versions in the per-demo repositories are the right destination.

The framing for the trio (generator + cell simulator + scanner) is the trio white paper. The encoder-brain bundle is not part of the trio — it is a parallel index that points to the trio members and to several other framework demos.

How to use it — demo by demo

Open the landing page and pick a demo. The bundled files:

  • 2026-03-23_-cell_simulator-NM_v01.html — small cells on a non-grid graph, each with local rules, scored by Bach BWV 847; emerges into HPA-axis-flavoured visuals. (Canonical live version: drt-cell-simulator.)
  • 2026-03-23_-generator-NM_v01.html — a discrete pipeline (rotating clock → lookup table → routing matrix → weighted nodes) shown side-by-side with the equivalent deep-graph unification. (Canonical: drt-generator.)
  • 2026-03-23_-scanner-NM_v01.html — the dual of the generator: signal flows from a master back through the columns, used for inspecting reconstruction quality. (Canonical: drt-scanner.)
  • 2026-03-23_-harmonic_solver-NM_v01.html — a small multi-agent debate visualization.
  • 2026-03-23_-pipeline-NM_v01.html — composer/decomposer pair running in parallel on the C-minor fugue, BWV 847. (Canonical: drt-pipeline.)
  • 2026-03-23_-supernova_solver-NM_v01.html — observers on orbital paths, with reconstruction at convergence points. (Canonical: drt-orbital-solver.)
  • CityDRT_2026-03-23_NM_v01.html — early sketch of the framework on a city topology.
  • GridDRT-Demo_2026-03-23_NM_v01.html — early sketch on a power-grid topology.
  • GridDRT_2026-03-23_NM_v01.java — a Java sketch of the grid version (not browser-runnable; provided as a code reference).
  • rank-sphere.html plus dated variants (_v05, _v06) — the rank-by-rank navigable sphere used by the decoder family.

Each file is independent; no shared CSS, no shared JavaScript, no asset directory. Click a file in the landing page to open it in your browser.

Reading the bundle

The bundled snapshots and the canonical live versions differ in several ways. Knowing which to read for what:

  • Use the bundled snapshots to scan the landscape quickly, see the breadth of demos in one place, or look at a historical record of how the ideas were being prototyped before the v0.1 paper.
  • Use the canonical live versions for the most up-to-date framing, the cleanest styling, the post-norm-growth-retraction prose, and the per-demo whitepapers.

For most readers, the canonical live versions are the right destination. The bundle is the index that points to them.

A note on framing changes between the bundled snapshots and the canonical live versions: the canonical versions reflect the v0.1 retraction of the norm-growth claim. The bundled snapshots predate that retraction in some places and may carry the older language. The conceptual content is the same in either version; the precise wording of the framework’s claims is more accurate in the canonical live versions and in the per-demo whitepapers linked from them.

Companion repositories — the canonical live versions

Several of the demos in this bundle have graduated into their own dedicated repositories with updated framing and per-repo whitepapers:

  • drt-generator — the canonical 7-column generator with whitepaper and presentation deck.
  • drt-scanner — the canonical scanner (transpose pass) with whitepaper.
  • drt-cell-simulator — the canonical cell simulator with whitepaper, plus a bio digital twin prototype with infinite LOD zoom at digital_twin.html.

The bundle is the historical sandbox; the dedicated repositories are the current state.

What is not in the paper

The honest list of what this paper deliberately does not claim:

  • A unified framework demo. The bundle is a collection of small isolated demos, not a single integrated system. Reading the bundle is like reading lab notebooks: each demo isolates one slice; the slices do not share code, styling, or runtime.
  • A current-state representation. The bundled HTMLs are dated 2026-03-23 and predate the v0.1 retraction of the norm-growth claim. Some bundled prose may carry the older language. The canonical live versions in the dedicated repositories are the up-to-date source.
  • A norm-growth claim. Earlier prose around this family of demos asserted that the aggregate of many partial views had bigger norm than the original signal — that ∥R(f)∥ > ∥f∥. That inequality has been retracted in the v0.1 draft. The current claim is conditional and weaker: under four hypotheses and a bounded computational budget, the aggregate exposes structural features that are not accessible from the original signal alone within the same budget. The bundled snapshots may not reflect this update; the canonical live versions do.
  • A production system. None of the bundled HTMLs is engineered for production use. They are visualizations. The framework’s actual computational substrate is in the dagdb-engine and savanna-engine repositories, not here.
